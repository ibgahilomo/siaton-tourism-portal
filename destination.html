Grandview Cottages Resort

Price:

Grandview Cottages Resort in Siaton is a comfortable accommodation in the southern parts of Negros Oriental that takes pride in its stunning landscape and amazing view of the sea.

Guests can fill their hearts with delight as they loiter around the wide green lawn brimming with life and lushness. It is fully ornamented with a garden of colorful flowering plants. If lucky enough, you can also see them in their full blossoms.

Antulang Beach Resort

Price:

Starts at ₱ 1500

There is only one word to describe this place – breathtaking! 40 kilometers south of Dumaguete City, Antulang Beach Resort lies suspended on a ten sprawling hectares of sheer cliffs. Their swimming pools and premium rooms are positioned right on the cliff's edge, ensuring guests a commanding view of an endless sea. The winding staircases lead directly to a kilometer's stretch of isolated white sand beach strewn with large, natural rock formations.



Beaches

Hidden Beach

Location: Salag, Siaton, Negros Oriental

Hidden Beach in Siaton is a pristine pocket of coastline just over an hour's drive from Dumaguete City. We stumbled upon this little gem as we drove aimlessly along the coast in search of the best beaches near Dumaguete to hang out just the two of us. Tucked away at the bottom of the cliff, this white sandy beach is dotted with giant boulders and tropical trees so we could lay a towel down in the shade. It might not be the biggest beach we've been to, but it was nice to have the place all to ourselves for an afternoon of sunbathing and swimming.

Turtle Island

Location: In front of Si-it, Siaton, Negros Oriental

The Turtle Island, located about 15 kilometres from the Siaton Municipal Plaza, has one of the best beaches in the Municipality. It can be reached via a habal-habal ride and then a short boat ride. The Turtle Island of Siaton got its name because of the turtles that hatch at a certain area during their season. The sands of the island are characterized with fine white sand on the other side and rough rocks on the other (during low tide).
